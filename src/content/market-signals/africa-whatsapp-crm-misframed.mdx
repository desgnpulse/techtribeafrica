A Reddit post appears on r/CRM in June 2026. The question: a CRM recommendation for a micro-business with no website, selling 100 percent via WhatsApp. Twenty-two replies arrive. They recommend Zoho, HubSpot, Pipedrive. None of them addresses the core constraint. The business has no email pipeline, no deal stages, no inbound web form. It has WhatsApp conversations. The question was answered. The problem was not.

---

The MEA CRM market is worth $3.92 billion in 2024. It is headed to $9.05 billion by 2030, growing at 15.4 percent annually. SMEs are the fastest-growing segment. The market numbers are not wrong. The category assumption is.

CRM as a product category was built for a specific transition. A business moves from paper records and offline processes to digital systems. The CRM tracks the pipeline, logs the call, moves the deal stage. For most African micro-businesses, those assumptions do not apply. There was no offline process to digitize. The business started on WhatsApp.

Seventy-eight percent of Sub-Saharan African SMEs run their sales through WhatsApp. The Reddit question about CRM for a WhatsApp-only business is not an edge case. In this market, it is the modal case.

---

African founders are building for this reality: DuceCRM, CRM Africa, Sukhiba, Ghala. The category names say CRM. The actual product is different. It is a structure layer on top of WhatsApp conversations. A system for turning unread messages into records a business can act on.

The naming carries risk. Calling the product a CRM imports the wrong expectation. The buyer searches "WhatsApp CRM Africa" and finds Salesforce documentation. The builder names the product CRM and spends cycles differentiating from tools built for a different market.

Nzube Ezudo named the structural gap on X in May 2026. The richest dataset on Nigerian-African SME businesses, he wrote, sits in WhatsApp - unstructured and unusable. The market is not waiting for more software. It is waiting for builders who do not start with the CRM mental model.
