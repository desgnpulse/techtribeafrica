📘 TECHTRIBEAFRICA.COM — PRD v1
“Narrative Intelligence for African Tech”
1. Product Summary

TechTribe Africa is a curated media platform that publishes insight-driven analysis of African and global tech trends, focusing on what they mean, not just what happened.

It sits between:

Tech news sites (Disrupt Africa, TechCabal-style reporting)
Research reports (Partech, Frontier Brief style analysis)
Indie commentary (Reddit, Hacker News discourse)

But it does something different:

It translates tech signals into economic, product, and founder-level implications for Africa.

2. The Core Thesis

African tech media is fragmented into three broken layers:

News Layer → What happened (VC rounds, launches)
Noise Layer → Twitter, LinkedIn opinions, shallow takes
Research Layer → Too slow, too academic, too disconnected
TechTribe fills the missing layer:

Signal → Meaning → Opportunity

This is the wedge.

3. Target Users (ICP)
Primary ICP

Builders & operators in African tech

Founders (pre-seed to Series A)
Indie hackers
Product managers
Early-stage investors
Technical operators in SMEs

They all share one behavior:

They are trying to understand “what to build next” or “what is changing.”

4. Core User Problem

Users are overwhelmed by:

Fragmented news sources
Repetitive startup announcements
Lack of context for African markets
Imported Silicon Valley narratives that don’t translate locally
Core pain:

“I see what is happening, but I don’t understand what it means for Africa.”

5. Positioning
One-line positioning:

TechTribe Africa is where African tech signals become actionable insight.

Alternative positioning:

We explain what African tech news actually means.

Category:

Narrative Intelligence Media Platform

(Not a news site. Not a blog. Not a newsletter aggregator.)

6. Content Strategy

TechTribe operates on 3 content pillars:

1. Frontier Reports (Deep Analysis)

Long-form essays (800–2000 words)

Examples:

WhatsApp CRM Pricing Shock & SME Impact
The Missing Middle of African SaaS
AI Infrastructure Race in Africa
Why Micro-business Software is the Real Opportunity Layer

Goal: Thought leadership + evergreen traffic

2. Signal Briefs (Short Analysis)

Short posts (200–600 words)

Examples:

“What 12 startup funding rounds actually signal”
“Why everyone is suddenly building AI agents in Africa”
“What Meta’s WhatsApp pricing change really breaks”

Goal: High frequency, high shareability

3. Build Opportunities (High Value Layer)

This is the moat.

Examples:

“5 underserved African SaaS markets nobody is building for”
“Where WhatsApp-native startups will win next”
“The $50/month SME software gap in Africa”

Goal: Attract builders, founders, and ideators
