African importers are settling $200 billion in trade annually through stablecoin OTC desks. They are calling brokers, not opening apps.

This is not a distribution problem. It is a signal about what the product needs to be.

When a Lagos textile importer needs $300,000 in USDT to clear a container from Guangzhou, they call a broker they have worked with for two years. They negotiate a rate, send naira, and receive USDT in two hours. No platform. No KYC form. A relationship.

The formal on-ramp landscape - Yellow Card, Paychant, SCRYPT - dominates institutional API volume and retail consumer transactions. None has captured the $50,000-to-$500,000 range where most importer demand lives. That range requires trust, not technology.

Accrue built around this in Lagos. Not an app. A human agent network. Physical people who hold float and process cash-to-stablecoin conversions for business clients. It raised $1.58 million in seed in mid-2026 and has processed $70-100 million.

The product that wins this market formalizes the broker relationship without removing the trust that makes it work. The question for African fintech founders: does that product get built by a new entrant or does an existing OTC desk acquire the compliance layer?
