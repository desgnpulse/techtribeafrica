# LinkedIn Post - africa-stablecoin-b2b-trade-finance

127 African financial institutions lost correspondent banking relationships in 2024-2025. The banks that cut them are not coming back.

Africa's trade finance gap now sits at $74 billion annually. It is projected to reach $86-$102 billion by 2027. That gap is not a liquidity shortage. It is a permanent structural exit. Global banks have calculated that compliance costs for smaller African institutions exceed the revenue.

What is filling the gap? B2B stablecoin settlement.

Monthly B2B stablecoin payment volume grew from under $100 million in early 2023 to over $6 billion by mid-2025. That is 60 times growth in 30 months. The cost structure explains it: 0.1-0.5% on stablecoin rails versus 2-7% through a correspondent chain.

This is not a remittance story. The shift that matters is in B2B trade settlement. Unit sizes are larger. Counterparties are known businesses. Transaction frequency is predictable. That makes stablecoin infrastructure more defensible than consumer use cases.

Ripple joined Flutterwave's Series E in June to build this infrastructure. Scrypt expanded stablecoin settlement rails across East Africa in July. African payment providers are bypassing SWIFT on specific corridors.

The question for African fintech builders is not whether stablecoin rails will replace correspondent banking on these corridors. They already are. The question is who builds the on-ramp, the trade finance instruments, and the treasury management layer on top.
